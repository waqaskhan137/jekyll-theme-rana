You are reading the demo of jekyll-theme-rana. The most important feature is the page you are
looking at right now: a post opens as a clean reading column. The sidebar, search, table of
contents, tag chips, share row, and related-post cards are all gone. The text sets in Charter,
a system serif, at roughly 20 pixels with generous line height. The only navigation left is the
arrow at the top, which takes you back to the post list.

The theme is for blogs where the writing is the product. List pages keep the full layout
(sidebar, search, dark mode toggle), so the site still feels like a site. Posts feel like pages
of a book.

Everything else is inherited from Chirpy: dark mode, search, archives, SEO tags, PWA support,
and the i18n locales. If you know Chirpy, you know how to configure this.
